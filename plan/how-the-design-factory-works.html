How the Design Factory works

A simple way to turn your raw inputs — a brief, your brand guidelines, call notes, a few links — into finished, on-brand design work, produced quickly and checked by a person before it ever reaches a client.

This is a walk through the flow, not the technology: what goes in, how it is processed, who does what, and what comes out. It covers websites and landing pages, Business Information Packs, LinkedIn carousels and banners, magazines and editorial, and email newsletters — with real examples from your own brand further down.

The system does the heavy lifting. A person owns the final 20% — taste, accuracy, and approval.

Nothing is delivered without a human signing off on it first. The bar is not “a computer made it” — it is “a discerning client would happily pay for it.”

The four stages, explained

What actually happens at each step

1

Intake — we capture everything

You hand over whatever you have: a brief, brand guidelines, your logo, website copy, call recordings, example sites you like. Nothing needs to be polished or organised.

This is the only step that needs your time.

briefbrand guidelogo + assetscall notesreference links
2

Digest — we turn it into truth

Every fact, number and brand detail is pulled into a single source of truth and a brand playbook — your colours, fonts, voice, and the things that make you distinctly you.

The rule: nothing is invented. If a statistic, logo or claim is not in what you gave us, it does not go on the page.

fact bankbrand playbookwhat is missing (flagged, not guessed)
3

Strategy — we set the approach

Before any design happens, we decide the creative direction: the message, the structure, and the one distinctive idea that makes the piece feel like Automate Accelerator and not a template anyone could have made.

the anglethe structurethe distinctive move
4

Production — the specialist builds it

The job is routed to the studio that owns that format — web, documents, social, editorial or email — plus an image team for any custom visuals. Several pieces can be produced at the same time.

the right studiocustom imagerymany pieces in parallel

The guardrail — quality check + human sign-off

Every finished piece runs through an automatic quality check that catches anything off-brand, broken, or unverified — including any number that does not trace back to a real fact. Then a person reviews and signs off. Only after both does it reach you. This is what makes the output consistent and safe to put in front of your clients.
